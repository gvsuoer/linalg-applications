Activity 35.9.

Let \(V\) be an inner product space of dimension \(n\text{,}\) and let \(W\) be a subspace of \(V\text{.}\) Let \(\vx\) be any vector in \(V\text{.}\) We will demonstrate that \(\vx\) can be written uniquely as a sum of a vector in \(W\) and a vector in \(W^{\perp}\text{.}\)

(a)

Explain why \(\proj_W \vx\) is in \(W\text{.}\)

(b)

Explain why \(\proj_{W^{\perp}} \vx\) is in \(W^{\perp}\text{.}\)

(c)

Explain why \(\vx\) can be written as a sum of vectors, one in \(W\) and one in \(W^{\perp}\text{.}\)

(d)

Now we demonstrate the uniqueness of this decomposition. Suppose \(\vx = \vw+\vw_1\) and \(\vx = \vu+\vu_1\text{,}\) where \(\vw\) and \(\vu\) are in \(W\) and \(\vw_1\) and \(\vu_1\) are in \(W^{\perp}\text{.}\) Show that \(\vw=\vu\) and \(\vw_1 = \vu_1\text{,}\) so that the representation of \(\vx\) as a sum of a vector in \(W\) and a vector in \(W^{\perp}\) is unique. (Hint: What is \(W \cap W^{\perp}\text{?}\))
