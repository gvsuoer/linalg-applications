We begin by discussing the row echelon or, simply, echelon form of a matrix. We know that the forward phase of the elimination on a linear system produces a system which can be solved by back substitution. The matrix representation of such a simplified system is said to be in row echelon or simply echelon form. Note that matrices in this form have the first nonzero entry in each row to the right of and below the first nonzero entry in the preceding row. Our next step is to formally describe this form — one that you tried to explain in problem 3 of Preview Activity 3.1.
