The Rank-Nullity Theorem (TheoremÂ 15.5) states that the rank plus the nullity of a matrix equals the number of columns of the matrix. There is a similar result for linear transformations that we prove in this exercise. Let \(T : V \to W\) be a linear transformation from an \(n\) dimensional vector space \(V\) to an \(m\) dimensional vector space \(W\text{.}\) Show that \(\dim(\Ker(T)) + \dim(\Range(T)) = n\text{.}\) (Hint: Let \(\{\vv_1, \vv_2, \ldots, \vv_k\}\) be a basis for \(\Ker(T)\text{.}\) Extend this basis to a basis \(\{\vv_1\text{,}\) \(\vv_2\text{,}\) \(\ldots\text{,}\) \(\vv_k\text{,}\) \(\vv_{k+1}\text{,}\) \(\ldots\text{,}\) \(\vv_n\}\) of \(V\text{.}\) Use this basis to find a basis for \(\Range(T)\text{.}\))
