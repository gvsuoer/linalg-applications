Let \(A\) be an \(n \times n\) matrix with eigenvalues \(\lambda_1\text{,}\) \(\lambda_2\text{,}\) \(\ldots\text{,}\) \(\lambda_n\) (not necessarily distinct). Exercise 1 in Chapter 18 shows that

\begin{equation} \det(A) = \lambda_1 \lambda_2 \cdots \lambda_n\text{.}\tag{27.7} \end{equation}
