The expression

\begin{equation*} b_1C_{1j} + b_2C_{2j} + \cdots + b_nC_{nj} \end{equation*}

is the cofactor expansion of the matrix

\begin{equation*} A_j = \left[ \begin{array}{cccc>{\columncolor{Gray}}cccc} a_{11} \amp a_{12} \amp \cdots \amp a_{1j-1} \amp \boldsymbol{b_1} \amp a_{1j+1} \amp \cdots \amp a_{1n} \\ a_{21} \amp a_{22} \amp \cdots \amp a_{2j-1} \amp \boldsymbol{b_2} \amp a_{2j+1} \amp \cdots \amp a_{2n} \\ \vdots \amp \vdots \amp \amp \amp \amp \amp \amp \vdots \\ a_{n1} \amp a_{n2} \amp \cdots \amp a_{nj-1} \amp \boldsymbol{b_n} \amp a_{nj+1} \amp \cdots \amp a_{nn} \end{array} \right] \end{equation*}

along the \(j\)th column, giving us the formula in Cramer's Rule.
