Exercise 19.

Label each of the following statements as True or False. Provide justification for your response.

(a) True/False.

An inner product on a vector space \(V\) is a function from \(V\) to the real numbers.

Answer.

(b) True/False.

If \(\langle \ , \ \rangle\) is an inner product on a vector space \(V\text{,}\) and if \(\vv\) is a vector in \(V\text{,}\) then the set \(W = \{\vx \in V : \langle \vx,\vv \rangle = 0\}\) is a subspace of \(V\text{.}\)

(c) True/False.

There is exactly one inner product on each inner product space.

Answer.

(d) True/False.

If \(\vx\text{,}\) \(\vy\text{,}\) and \(\vz\) are vectors in an inner product space with \(\langle \vx, \vz \rangle = \langle \vy, \vz \rangle\text{,}\) then \(\vx = \vy\text{.}\)

(e) True/False.

If \(\langle \vx, \vy \rangle = 0\) for all vectors \(\vy\) in an inner product space \(V\text{,}\) then \(\vx = \vzero\text{.}\)

Answer.

(f) True/False.

If \(\vu\) and \(\vv\) are vectors in an inner product space and the distance from \(\vu\) to \(\vv\) is the same as the distance from \(\vu\) to \(-\vv\text{,}\) then \(\vu\) and \(\vv\) are orthogonal.

(g) True/False.

If \(W\) is a subspace of an inner product space and a vector \(\vv\) is orthogonal to every vector in a basis of \(W\text{,}\) then \(\vv\) is in \(W^{\perp}\text{.}\)

Answer.

(h) True/False.

If \(\{\vv_1, \vv_2, \vv_3\}\) is an orthogonal basis for an inner product space \(V\text{,}\) then so is \(\{c\vv_1, \vv_2, \vv_3\}\) for any nonzero scalar \(c\text{.}\)

(i) True/False.

An inner product \(\langle \vu, \vv \rangle\) in an inner product space \(V\) results in another vector in \(V\text{.}\)

Answer.

(j) True/False.

An inner product in an inner product space \(V\) is a function that maps pairs of vectors in \(V\) to the set of non-negative real numbers.

(k) True/False.

The vector space of all \(n \times n\) matrices can be made into an inner product space.

Answer.

(l) True/False.

Any non-zero multiple of an inner product on space \(V\) is also an inner product on \(V\text{.}\)

(m) True/False.

Every set of \(k\) non-zero orthogonal vectors in a vector space \(V\) of dimension \(k\) is a basis for \(V\text{.}\)

Answer.

(n) True/False.

For any finite-dimensional inner product space \(V\) and a subspace \(W\) of \(V\text{,}\) \(W\) is a subspace of \((W^\perp)^\perp\text{.}\)

(o) True/False.

If \(W\) is a subspace of an inner product space, then \(W\cap W^\perp = \{\vzero\}\text{.}\)

Answer.
