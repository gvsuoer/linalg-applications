Exercise 9.

Exercise 1 in Chapter 18 shows that the determinant of a matrix is the product of its eigenvalues. In this exercise we show that the trace of a diagonalizable matrix is the sum of its eigenvalues. 33  First we define the trace of a matrix.

Definition 19.8.

The trace of an \(n \times n\) matrix \(A = [a_{ij}]\) is the sum of the diagonal entries of \(A\text{.}\) That is,

\begin{equation*} \trace(A) = a_{11} + a_{22} + \cdots + a_{nn} = \sum_{i=1}^n a_{ii}\text{.} \end{equation*}

(a)

Show that if \(R = [r_{ij}]\) and \(S = [s_{ij}]\) are \(n \times n\) matrices, then \(\trace(RS) = \trace(SR)\text{.}\)

Answer.
