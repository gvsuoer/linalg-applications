Exercise 8.

In this exercise we analyze all inner products on \(\R^n\text{.}\) Let \(\langle \ , \ \rangle\) be an inner product on \(\R^n\text{.}\) Let \(\vx = [x_1 \ x_2 \ \ldots \ x_n]^{\tr}\) and \(\vy = [y_1 \ y_2 \ \ldots \ y_n]^{\tr}\) be arbitrary vectors in \(\R^n\text{.}\) Then

\begin{equation*} \vx = \sum_{i=1}^n x_i \ve_i \ \ \ \text{ and } \ \ \ \vy = \sum_{j=1}^n y_j \ve_j\text{,} \end{equation*}

where \(\ve_i\) is the \(i\)th standard vector in \(\R^n\text{.}\)

(a)

Explain why

\begin{equation} \langle \vx, \vy \rangle = \sum_{i=1}^n \sum_{j=1}^n x_i \langle \ve_i, \ve_j \rangle y_j\text{.}\tag{28.3} \end{equation}
(b)

Calculate the matrix product

\begin{equation*} \vx^{\tr} \left[ \begin{array}{cccc} \langle \ve_1,\ve_1 \rangle \amp \langle \ve_1,\ve_2 \rangle \amp \cdots \amp \langle \ve_1,\ve_n \rangle \\ \langle \ve_2,\ve_1 \rangle \amp \langle \ve_2,\ve_2 \rangle \amp \cdots \amp \langle \ve_2,\ve_n \rangle \\ \vdots \amp \vdots \amp \amp \vdots \\ \langle \ve_n,\ve_1 \rangle \amp \langle \ve_n,\ve_2 \rangle \amp \cdots \amp \langle \ve_n,\ve_n \rangle \end{array} \right] \vy \end{equation*}

and compare to (28.3). What do you notice?

(c)

Explain why any inner product on \(\R^n\) is of the form \(\vx^{\tr} A \vy\) for some symmetric, positive definite matrix \(A\text{.}\)
