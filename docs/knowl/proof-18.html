Proof

Let \(W\) be a subspace of an inner product space \(V\) and let \(\vu\) be a vector in \(V\text{.}\) Let \(\vx\) be a vector in \(W\text{.}\) Now

\begin{equation*} \vu - \vx = (\vu-\proj_W \vu) + (\proj_W \vu - \vx)\text{.} \end{equation*}

Since both \(\proj_W \vu\) and \(\vx\) are in \(W\text{,}\) we know that \(\proj_W \vu - \vx\) is in \(W\text{.}\) Since \(\proj_{W^{\perp}} \vu = \vu-\proj_W \vu\) is orthogonal to every vector in \(W\text{,}\) we have that \(\vu-\proj_W \vu\) is orthogonal to \(\proj_W \vu - \vx\text{.}\) We can now use the Generalized Pythagorean Theorem to conclude that

\begin{equation*} ||\vu - \vx||^2 = ||\vu-\proj_W \vu||^2 + ||\proj_W \vu - \vx||^2\text{.} \end{equation*}

Since \(\vx \neq \proj_W \vu\text{,}\) it follows that \(||\proj_W \vu - \vx||^2 > 0\) and

\begin{equation*} || \vu - \vx ||^2 > ||\vu - \proj_W \vu ||^2\text{.} \end{equation*}

Since norms are nonnegative, we can conclude that \(||\vu - \proj_W \vu || \lt || \vu - \vx ||\) as desired.
