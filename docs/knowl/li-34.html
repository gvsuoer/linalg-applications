Item 1.

Resistance \(R\) in ohms (\(\Omega\)) can be thought of as a measure of how difficult it is to move a charge along a circuit. When a current flows through a resistor, it must expend some energy, called a voltage drop. Ohm's Law states that the voltage drop \(E\) across a resistor is the product of the current \(I\) passing through the resistor and the resistance \(R\text{.}\) That is,

\begin{equation*} E = IR\text{.} \end{equation*}
