Exercise 7.

Suppose we have

\begin{equation*} \left[ \begin{array}{rccc} 1 \amp 2 \amp 1 \amp 2\\-1 \amp 2 \amp 3 \amp 1\\2\amp 3 \amp 1 \amp a \end{array} \right] \left[ \begin{array}{r} 1 \\ 2 \\ -2 \\ 3 \end{array} \right] = \left[ \begin{array}{c} b_1 \\ b_2\\ b_3 \end{array} \right] \end{equation*}

where \(b_i\)'s represent unknown values.

(a)

In order to find the value of \(a\text{,}\) which of the \(b_i\)'s do we need to know? Why?

(b)

Suppose the \(b_i\)(s) that we need to know is(are) equal to 9. What is the value of \(a\text{?}\)
