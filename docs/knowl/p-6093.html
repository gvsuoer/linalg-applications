In ChapterÂ 25 we showed that in the inner product space \(\R^n\) using the dot product as inner product, if \(W\) is a subspace of \(\R^n\) and \(\vv\) is in \(\R^n\text{,}\) then \(\proj_{W^{\perp}} \vv\) is orthogonal to every vector in \(W\text{.}\) In this activity we verify that same fact in an inner product space. That is, assume that \(\CB = \{\vw_1, \vw_2, \ldots, \vw_m\}\) is an orthogonal basis for a subspace \(W\) of an inner product space \(V\) and \(\vv\) is a vector in \(V\text{.}\) Follow the indicated steps to show that \(\proj_{W^{\perp}} \vv\) is orthogonal to every vector in \(\CB\text{.}\)
