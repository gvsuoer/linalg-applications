Item.

We add two vectors of the same size by adding corresponding components. In other words, if \(\vu\) and \(\vv\) are vectors of the same size and \(u_i\) and \(v_i\) are the \(i\) components of \(\vu\) and \(\vv\text{,}\) respectively, then \(\vu +\vv\) is the vector whose \(i\)th component is \(u_i+v_i\) for each \(i\text{.}\) Geometrically, we represent the sum of two vectors using the Parallelogram Rule: The vector \(\vu+\vv\) is the directed line segment from the origin to the 4th point of the parallelogram formed by the origin and the vectors \(\vu, \vv\text{.}\)
