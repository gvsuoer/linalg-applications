Exercise 14.

Let \(\pol_2\) be the inner product space with inner product

\begin{equation*} \langle p(t), q(t) \rangle = \int_0^1 p(t)q(t) \ dt\text{.} \end{equation*}

Let \(\CB = \{1, 1-2t\}\) and let \(W = \Span \ \CB\) in \(\pol_2\text{.}\)

(a)

Show that \(\CB\) is an orthogonal basis for \(W\text{,}\) using the given inner product.

(b)

Explain why the polynomial \(q(t) = t^2\) is not in \(W\text{.}\)

(c)

Find the vector in \(W\) that is closest to \(q(t)\text{.}\) How close is this vector to \(q(t)\text{?}\)
