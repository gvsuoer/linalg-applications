Answer 7.d.iii.1.

For \(t=0\text{,}\) we have \(Q(t) = (4-t)t-4)(t-6)\text{,}\) and \([6 \ -6 \ 0]^{\tr}\) an eigenvector for \(A\) with eigenvalue \(0\text{.}\) For \(t=4\) we have \(Q(t) = t(t-6)\text{,}\) and \([24 \ 24 \ 0]^{\tr}\) is an eigenvector for \(A\) with eigenvalue \(4\text{.}\) For \(t=6\) we have \(Q(t) = t(t-4)\text{,}\) and \([0 \ -6 \ -12]^{\tr}\) is an eigenvector for \(A\) with eigenvalue \(6\text{.}\)
