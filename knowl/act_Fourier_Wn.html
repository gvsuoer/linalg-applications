Project Activity 35.12.

A table of integrals shows the following for \(k \neq m\) (up to a constant):

\begin{align} \int \cos(mx)\cos(kx) \ dx \amp = \frac{1}{2} \left( \frac{\sin((k-m)x)}{k-m} + \frac{\sin((k+m)x)}{k+m} \right)\tag{35.4}\\ \int \sin(mx)\sin(kx) \ dx \amp = \frac{1}{2} \left( \frac{\sin((k-m)x)}{k-m} - \frac{\sin((k+m)x)}{k+m} \right)\tag{35.5}\\ \int \cos(mx)\sin(kx) \ dx \amp = \frac{1}{2} \left( \frac{\cos((m-k)x)}{m-k} - \frac{\sin((m+k)x)}{m+k} \right)\tag{35.6}\\ \int \cos(mx)\sin(mx) \ dx \amp = -\frac{1}{2m} \cos^2(mx)\tag{35.7} \end{align}
(a)

Use (35.4) to show that \(\cos(mx)\) and \(\cos(kx)\) are orthogonal in \(C[-\pi,\pi]\) if \(k \neq m\text{.}\)

(b)

Use (35.5) to show that \(\sin(mx)\) and \(\sin(kx)\) are orthogonal in \(C[-\pi,\pi]\) if \(k \neq m\text{.}\)

(c)

Use (35.6) to show that \(\cos(mx)\) and \(\sin(kx)\) are orthogonal in \(C[-\pi,\pi]\) if \(k \neq m\text{.}\)

(d)

Use (35.7) to show that \(\cos(mx)\) and \(\sin(mx)\) are orthogonal in \(C[-\pi,\pi]\text{.}\)

(e)

What is \(\dim(W_n)\text{?}\) Explain.
