Now we return to our inner product space \(C[-\pi,\pi]\) with inner product (35.3). Given a function \(g\) in \(C[-\pi,\pi]\text{,}\) we approximate \(g\) using only a finite number of the terms in a trigonometric polynomial. Let \(W_n\) be the subspace of \(C[-\pi,\pi]\) spanned by the functions

\begin{equation*} 1, \cos(x), \sin(x), \cos(2x), \sin(2x), \ldots, \cos(nx), \sin(nx)\text{.} \end{equation*}
