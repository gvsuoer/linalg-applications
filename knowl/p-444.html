Since every elementary row operation is reversible, if \(B\) is row equivalent to \(A\text{,}\) then \(A\) is also row equivalent to \(B\text{.}\) Thus, we just say that \(A\) and \(B\) are row equivalent. While the row echelon form of a matrix is not unique, it is the case that the reduced row echelon form of a matrix is unique.
