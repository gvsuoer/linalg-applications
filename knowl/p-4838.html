Suppose \(A\) and \(B\) are symmetric \(n \times n\) matrices, and let \(Q_A(\vx) = \vx^{\tr}A\vx\) and \(Q_B(\vx) = \vx^{\tr} B\vx\text{.}\) If \(Q_A(\vx) = Q_B(\vx)\) for all \(\vx\) in \(\R^n\text{,}\) show that \(A=B\text{.}\) (Hint: Use ExerciseÂ 6 (a) to compare \(Q_A(\ve_i)\) and \(Q_B(\ve_i)\text{,}\) then compare \(Q_A(\ve_i+\ve_j)\) to \(Q_B(\ve_i + \ve_j)\) for \(i \neq j\text{.}\)) Thus, quadratic forms are uniquely determined by their symmetric matrices.
