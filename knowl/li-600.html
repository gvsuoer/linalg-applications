Item 3.

If any two successive entries in the first row of \(L\) are both positive, then \(|\lambda_i| \lt \lambda_1\) for every \(i > 1\text{.}\) In this case we say that \(\lambda_1\) is a strictly dominant eigenvalue of \(L\text{.}\) In a Leslie model, this happens when the females in two successive age classes are fertile, which is almost always the case.
