Let \(n\) be a positive integer, and let \(\CB\text{,}\) \(\CC\text{,}\) and \(\CS\) be bases for \(\R^n\text{.}\) Then

  1. the change of basis matrix \(\underset{\CC \leftarrow \CB}{P}\) is invertible,

  2. \(\underset{\CC \leftarrow \CB}{P}^{-1} = \underset{\CB \leftarrow \CC}{P}\text{,}\)

  3. \(\underset{\CS \leftarrow \CC}{P} \ \underset{\CC \leftarrow \CB}{P} = \underset{\CS \leftarrow \CB}{P}\text{.}\)
