Activity 10.2.

(a)

Let \(A = \left[ \begin{array}{cc} 1 \amp 0 \\ 0 \amp 0 \end{array} \right]\text{.}\) Calculate \(AB\) where \(B = \left[ \begin{array}{cc} a\amp b \\ c\amp d \end{array} \right]\text{.}\) Using your result, explain why it is not possible to have \(AB=I_2\text{,}\) showing that \(A\) is non-invertible.

(b)

Calculate \(AB\) where \(A = \left[ \begin{array}{cc} 1 \amp 2 \\ 2 \amp 4 \end{array} \right]\) and \(B = \left[ \begin{array}{cc} a\amp b \\ c\amp d \end{array} \right]\text{.}\) Using your result, explain why the inverse of \(A\) doesn't exist.
