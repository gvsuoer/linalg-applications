Let \(Q\) be the quadratic form defined by \(Q(\vx) = \vx^{\tr} A \vx\text{,}\) where \(A\) is an \(n \times n\) symmetric matrix. As in Preview ActivityÂ 28.1, the fact that \(A\) is symmetric means that we can find an orthogonal matrix \(P = [ \vp_1 \ \vp_2 \ \vp_3 \ \cdots \ \vp_n ]\) whose columns are orthonormal eigenvectors of \(A\) corresponding to eigenvalues \(\lambda_1\text{,}\) \(\lambda_2\text{,}\) \(\ldots\text{,}\) \(\lambda_n\text{,}\) respectively. Letting \(\vy = P^{\tr} \vx\) give us \(\vx = P\vy\) and

\begin{equation*} \vx^{\tr} A \vx = (P\vy)^{\tr}A(P\vy) = \vy^{\tr}(P^{\tr}AP)\vy = \vy^{\tr} D \vy\text{,} \end{equation*}

where \(D\) is the diagonal matrix whose \(i\)th diagonal entry is \(\lambda_i\text{.}\)
