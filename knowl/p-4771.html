The dot product and the example in Preview ActivityÂ 28.3 provide two examples of inner products. The examples below provide two other important inner products on \(\R^n\text{.}\)

  • If \(a_1\text{,}\) \(a_2\text{,}\) \(\ldots\text{,}\) \(a_n\) are positive scalars, then

    \begin{equation*} \langle [u_1 \ u_2 \ \cdots \ u_n]^{\tr}, [v_1 \ v_2 \ \cdots \ v_n]^{\tr} \rangle = a_1u_1v_1+a_2u_2v_2+ \cdots + a_nu_nv_n \end{equation*}

    defines an inner product on \(\R^n\text{.}\)

  • Every invertible \(n \times n\) matrix \(A\) defines an inner product on \(\R^n\) by

    \begin{equation*} \langle \vu, \vv \rangle = (A\vu) \cdot (A\vv)\text{.} \end{equation*}
