We can geometrically represent a vector \(\vv = \left[ \begin{array}{c} v_1\\ v_2 \end{array} \right]\) in \(\R^2\) as the point \((v_1, v_2)\) in the plane as we did in Preview ActivityÂ 4.1. We can similarly represent a vector \(\vv = \left[ \begin{array}{c} v_1\\ v_2\\ v_3 \end{array} \right]\) in \(\R^3\) as the point \((v_1, v_2, v_3)\) in the three-dimensional space. This geometric representation will be handy when we consider collections of infinitely many vectors, as we will do when we consider the span of a collection of vectors later in this section.
