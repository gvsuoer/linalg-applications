Suppose we are given a system of two linear equations

\begin{alignat}{4} {4} x_1 \amp {}+{} \amp 2x_2 \amp {}-{} \amp x_3{}={} \amp { } \amp 1\amp {}\tag{1.7}\\ 3x_1 \amp {}+{} \amp x_2 \amp {}+{} \amp 2x_3{}={} \amp {-} \amp 1\amp \text{.}\tag{1.8} \end{alignat}

Find another system of two linear equations \(E_1\) and \(E_2\) in the variables \(x_1\text{,}\) \(x_2\text{,}\) and \(x_3\) that are not multiples of each other or of equations (1.7) or (1.8) so that any solution \((x_1, x_2, x_3)\) to the system (1.7) and (1.8) is a solution to the system \(E_1\) and \(E_2\text{.}\)
