To understand where a knight can move in a chess game, we need to know the initial setup. A chess board is an \(8 \times 8\) grid. To be able to refer to the individual positions on the board, we will place the board so that its lower left corner is at the origin, make each square in the grid have side length \(1\text{,}\) and label each square with the point at the lower left corner.
