The coordinate transformation \(T\) is one-to-one, so it has an inverse \(T^{-1}\text{.}\) Let \(V\) be an \(n\)-dimensional vector space that has a basis \(\CB\text{,}\) and let \(T : V \to \R^n\) be the coordinate transformation defined by \(T(\vx) = [\vx]_{\CB}\text{.}\) Let \(S = \{\vu_1, \vu_2, \ldots, \vu_k\}\) be a subset of \(V\) and let \(R = \{[\vu_1]_{\CB}, [\vu_2]_{\CB}, \ldots, [\vu_k]_{\CB}\}\) in \(\R^n\text{.}\)
