When we connect the results of this example with the result of ExerciseÂ 5 in this section, we obtain a linear transformation analog of the Rank-Nullity Theorem. That is, if \(T\) is a linear transformation from a vector space \(V\) of dimension \(n\) with basis \(\CB\) to a vector space \(W\) of dimension \(m\) with basis \(\CC\text{,}\) then

\begin{equation*} \dim(\Ker(T)) + \dim(\Range(T)) = \dim(\Nul \ [T]_{\CB}^{\CC}) + \dim(\Col \ [T]_{\CB}^{\CC}) = n\text{.} \end{equation*}
