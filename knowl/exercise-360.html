Exercise 13.

The coordinate transformation \(T\) is one-to-one, so it has an inverse \(T^{-1}\text{.}\) Let \(V\) be an \(n\)-dimensional vector space that has a basis \(\CB\text{,}\) and let \(T : V \to \R^n\) be the coordinate transformation defined by \(T(\vx) = [\vx]_{\CB}\text{.}\) Let \(S = \{\vu_1, \vu_2, \ldots, \vu_k\}\) be a subset of \(V\) and let \(R = \{[\vu_1]_{\CB}, [\vu_2]_{\CB}, \ldots, [\vu_k]_{\CB}\}\) in \(\R^n\text{.}\)

(a)

Suppose \(\vx\) is in \(V\) with \(\vx = x_1 \vu_1 + x_2\vu_2 + \cdots + x_k \vu_k\text{.}\) Write the vector \([\vx]_{\CB}\) as a linear combination of the vectors in \(R\text{.}\) Explain your reasoning and explain how your result shows that \(T\) preserves linear combinations.

(b)

Now suppose \(\vw\) is in \(V\) so that \([\vw]_{\CB} = w_1 [\vu_1]_{\CB} + w_2[\vu_2]_{\CB} + \cdots + w_k [\vu_k]_{\CB}\text{.}\) Write the vector \(\vw\) as a linear combination of the vectors in \(S\text{.}\) Explain your reasoning and explain how your result shows that \(T^{-1}\) preserves linear combinations.
