Preview Activity 35.1.

Consider the vector space \(\pol_2\) of polynomials of degree less than or equal to \(2\) with real coefficients. Define a mapping from \(\pol_2 \times \pol_2\) to \(\R\) by

\begin{equation*} \langle p(t), q(t) \rangle = \int_0^1 p(t)q(t) \, dt\text{.} \end{equation*}
(a)

Calculate \(\langle 1, t \rangle\text{.}\)

(b)

If \(p(t)\) and \(q(t)\) are in \(\pol_2\text{,}\) is it true that

\begin{equation*} \langle p(t) , q(t) \rangle = \langle q(t), p(t) \rangle? \end{equation*}

Verify your answer.

(c)

If \(p(t)\text{,}\) \(q(t)\text{,}\) and \(r(t)\) are in \(\pol_2\text{,}\) is it true that

\begin{equation*} \langle p(t)+q(t), r(t) \rangle = \langle p(t), r(t) \rangle + \langle q(t), r(t) \rangle? \end{equation*}

Verify your answer.

(d)

If \(p(t)\) and \(q(t)\) are in \(\pol_2\) and \(c\) is a scalar, is it true that

\begin{equation*} \langle cp(t) , q(t) \rangle = c\langle p(t) , q(t) \rangle? \end{equation*}

Verify your answer.

(e)

If \(p(t)\) is in \(\pol_2\text{,}\) must it be the case that \(\langle p(t) , p(t) \rangle \geq 0\text{?}\) When is \(\langle p(t) , p(t) \rangle = 0\text{?}\)
