An inner product \(\langle \ , \ \rangle\) on \(\R^n\) is a mapping from \(\R^n \times \R^n \to \R\) satisfying

  1. \(\langle \vu , \vv \rangle = \langle \vv , \vu \rangle\) for all \(\vu\) and \(\vv\) in \(\R^n\text{,}\)

  2. \(\langle \vu + \vv , \vw \rangle = \langle \vu , \vw \rangle + \langle \vv , \vw \rangle\) for all \(\vu\text{,}\) \(\vv\text{,}\) and \(\vw\) in \(\R^n\text{,}\)

  3. \(\langle c\vu , \vv \rangle = c\langle \vu , \vv \rangle\) for all \(\vu\text{,}\) \(\vv\) in \(\R^n\) and all scalars \(c\text{,}\)

  4. \(\langle \vu , \vu \rangle \geq 0\) for all \(\vu\) in \(\R^n\) and \(\langle \vu , \vu \rangle = 0\) if and only if \(\vu = \vzero\text{.}\)
