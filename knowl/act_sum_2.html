Project Activity 34.7.

Consider the sum \(\sum_{t=0}^{n-1} t^2\text{.}\) We want to write \(t^2\) as a linear combination of \(p_0(t)\text{,}\) \(p_1(t)\) and \(p_2(t)\text{.}\) To do so, we will use the coordinate vectors with respect to \(\CS_2\) and do our work in \(\R^3\text{.}\)

(a)

Find \([p_0(t)]_{\CS_2}\text{,}\) \([p_1(t)]_{\CS_2}\text{,}\) \([p_2(t)]_{\CS_2}\text{,}\) and \([t^2]_{\CS_2}\text{.}\)

(b)

Use the coordinate vectors from part (a) to write \(t^2\) as a linear combination of the vectors in \(\CP_2\text{.}\)

(c)

Use the result of part (b) and, the hockey-stick identity, and the fact that \(p_k(t) = \binom{t}{k}\) to find a formula for \(\sum_{t=0}^{n-1} t^2\text{.}\)
