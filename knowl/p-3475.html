As sheep reproduce, they add to the 0-1 sheep (lamb) population. The potential to produce offspring is called fecundity (derived from the word fecund which generally refers to reproductive ability) and determines how many lamb are added to the population. Let \(F_k\) (the fecundity rate) be the rate at which females in age class \(k\) give birth to female offspring. Not all members of a given age group survive to the next age groups, so let \(s_k\) be the fraction of individuals that survives from age group \(k\) to age group \(k+1\text{.}\) With these ideas in mind, we can create a life cycle chart as in FigureÂ 20.9 that illustrates how the population of sheep changes on a farm (for the sake of space, we illustrate with four age classes).
