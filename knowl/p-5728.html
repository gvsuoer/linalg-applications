Now we have all of the necessary background to discuss image compression. Suppose we want to store an image. We partition the image vertically and horizontally and record the color or shade at each grid entry. The grid entries will be our pixels. This gives a matrix, \(M\text{,}\) of colors, indexed by pixels or horizontal and vertical position. To simplify our examples we will work in gray-scale, where our grid entries are integers between 0 (black) and 255 (white). We can treat each column of our grid as a piecewise constant function.
