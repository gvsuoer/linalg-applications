ActivityÂ 6.4 contains the general ideas to show that any vector in the span of a linearly independent set can be written in one and only one way as a linear combination of the vectors in the set. The weights of such a linear combination provide us a coordinate system for the vectors in terms of the basis. Two familiar concepts of coordinate systems are the Cartesian coordinates and \(xy\)-plane, and \(xyz\)-space. We will revisit the coordinate system idea in a later chapter.
