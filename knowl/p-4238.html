Preview ActivityÂ 25.1 gives an indication of how we can project a vector \(\vv\) in \(\R^n\) onto a subspace \(W\) of \(\R^n\text{.}\) If we have an orthogonal basis for \(W\text{,}\) we can just add the projections of \(\vv\) onto each basis vector. The resulting vector is called the projection of \(\vv\) onto the subspace \(W\text{.}\) As we did with projections onto vectors, we can also define the projection of \(\vv\) orthogonal to \(W\text{.}\) Note that to make this all work out properly, we will see that we need an orthogonal basis for \(W\text{.}\)
