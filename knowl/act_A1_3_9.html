Activity 4.5.

(a)

By definition, \(\Span\left\{\left[ \begin{array}{r} 1 \\ -2 \end{array} \right]\right\}\) is the collection of all vectors which are scalar multiples of \(\left[ \begin{array}{r} 1 \\ -2 \end{array} \right]\text{.}\) Determine which vectors are in this collection. If we plot all these vectors with each vector being represented as a point in the plane, what do they form?

(b)

Let \(\vv_1 = \left[ \begin{array}{c} 1 \\ 0\\ 1 \end{array} \right]\) and \(\vv_2 = \left[ \begin{array}{c} 0\\1 \\1 \end{array} \right]\) in \(\R^3\text{.}\) By definition,

\begin{equation*} \Span\left\{\left[ \begin{array}{c} 1 \\ 0\\ 1 \end{array} \right], \left[ \begin{array}{c} 0\\1 \\1 \end{array} \right] \right\} \end{equation*}

is the collection of all linear combinations of the form

\begin{equation*} x_1 \left[ \begin{array}{c} 1 \\ 0\\ 1 \end{array} \right] + x_2 \left[ \begin{array}{c} 0\\1 \\1 \end{array} \right]\text{,} \end{equation*}

where \(x_1\) and \(x_2\) are any scalars.

(i)

Find four different vectors in \(\Span \{\vv_1, \vv_2\}\) and indicate the weights (the values of \(x_1\) and \(x_2\)) for each linear combination.

Hint.
(ii)

Are there any vectors in \(\R^3\) that are not in \(\Span\{\vv_1, \vv_2\}\text{?}\) Explain. Verify your result.

(iii)

Set up a linear system to determine which vectors \(\vw= \left[ \begin{array}{c} w_1 \\ w_2\\ w_3 \end{array} \right]\) are in \(\Span\{\vv_1, \vv_2\}\text{.}\) Specifically, which \(\vw\) can be expressed as a linear combination of \(\vv_1\) and \(\vv_2\text{?}\)

(iv)

Geometrically, what shape do the vectors in \(\Span\{\vv_1, \vv_2\}\) form inside \(\R^3\text{?}\)

(c)

Is it possible for \(\Span\{\vz_1, \vz_2\}\) to be a line for two vectors \(\vz_1, \vz_2\) in \(\R^3\text{?}\)

(d)

What do you think are the possible geometric descriptions of a span of a set of vectors in \(\R^2\text{?}\) Explain.

(e)

What do you think are the possible spans of a set of vectors in \(\R^3\text{?}\) Explain.
