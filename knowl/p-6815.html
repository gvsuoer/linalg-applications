Preview ActivityÂ 39.1 presents a method for finding eigenvalues and eigenvectors of linear transformations. That is, if \(T: V \to V\) is a linear transformation and \(\CB\) is a basis for \(V\text{,}\) and if \(\vx\) is an eigenvector of \([T]_{\CB}\) with eigenvalue \(\lambda\text{,}\) then the vector \(\vv\) in \(V\) satisfying \([\vv]_{\CB}=\vx\) has the property that

\begin{equation*} [T(\vv)]_{\CB} = [T]_{\CB}[\vv]_{\CB} = [T]_{\CB} \vx = \lambda \vx = [\lambda \vv]_{\CB}\text{.} \end{equation*}
