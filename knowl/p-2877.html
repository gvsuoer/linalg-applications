With the change of basis matrices we can convert from any one coordinate system to the other. Note that all of the change of basis matrices are written in terms of angles, so it will be convenient to have a way to express points on our ellipses using angles as well. Given any point on an ellipse (or any point in the plane), we can represent the coordinates of that point in terms of the angle \(\theta\) the vector through the origin and the point makes with the positive \(x\)-axis and the distance \(r\) from the origin to the point as shown in FigureÂ 16.12. In this representation we have \(x = r\cos(\theta)\) and \(y = r \sin(\theta)\text{.}\)
