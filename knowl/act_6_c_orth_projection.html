Activity 35.6.

In Section 25 we showed that in the inner product space \(\R^n\) using the dot product as inner product, if \(W\) is a subspace of \(\R^n\) and \(\vv\) is in \(\R^n\text{,}\) then \(\proj_{W^{\perp}} \vv\) is orthogonal to every vector in \(W\text{.}\) In this activity we verify that same fact in an inner product space. That is, assume that \(\CB = \{\vw_1, \vw_2, \ldots, \vw_m\}\) is an orthogonal basis for a subspace \(W\) of an inner product space \(V\) and \(\vv\) is a vector in \(V\text{.}\) Follow the indicated steps to show that \(\proj_{W^{\perp}} \vv\) is orthogonal to every vector in \(\CB\text{.}\)

(a)

Let \(\vz\) be the projection of \(\vv\) onto \(W\text{.}\) Write \(\vz\) in terms of the basis vectors in \(\CB\text{.}\)

(b)

The vector \(\vv - \vz\) is the projection of \(\vv\) orthogonal to \(W\text{.}\) Let \(k\) be between \(1\) and \(m\text{.}\) Use the result of part (a) to show that \(\vv - \vz\) is orthogonal to \(\vw_k\text{.}\) Exercise 16 then shows that \(\vv - \vz\) is orthogonal to every vector in \(W\text{.}\)
