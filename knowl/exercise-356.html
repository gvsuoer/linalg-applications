Exercise 9.

Let \(\CB=\left\{ \left[\begin{array}{cc} 1\amp 0\\0\amp 0 \end{array} \right], \left[\begin{array}{cc} 1\amp 1\\0\amp 0 \end{array} \right], \left[\begin{array}{cc} 0\amp 0\\1\amp 1 \end{array} \right], \left[\begin{array}{cc} 1\amp 2\\2\amp 1 \end{array} \right] \right\}\) in \(M_{2\times 2}\text{.}\)

(a)

Show that \(\CB\) is a basis of \(M_{2\times 2}\text{.}\)

(b)

Let

\begin{equation*} A = \left[\begin{array}{cc} 1\amp 2\\2\amp 1 \end{array} \right] \, , \, B=\left[\begin{array}{cc} 1\amp 1\\1\amp 1 \end{array} \right] \, , \, C= \left[\begin{array}{cc} 2\amp 1\\1\amp 1 \end{array} \right]\, , \, D= \left[\begin{array}{cc} 1\amp 1\\1\amp 0 \end{array} \right]\text{.} \end{equation*}

Find \([A]_\CB,[B]_\CB, [C]_\CB, [D]_\CB\text{.}\)

(c)

Determine if the set \(\{A, B, C, D\}\) is linearly dependent or independent.
