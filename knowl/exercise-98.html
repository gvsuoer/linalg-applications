Exercise 3.

(a)

For a few different \(k\) values, find the inverse of \(A = \left[ \begin{array}{cc} 1\amp k \\ 0\amp 1 \end{array} \right]\text{.}\) From these results, make a conjecture as to what \(A^{-1}\) is in general.

(b)

Prove your conjecture using the definition of inverse matrix.

(c)

Find the inverse of \(A = \left[ \begin{array}{ccc} 1\amp k\amp \ell \\ 0\amp 1\amp m\\0\amp 0\amp 1 \end{array} \right]\text{.}\)

(Note: You can combine the first two parts above by applying the inverse finding algorithm directly on \(A = \left[ \begin{array}{cc} 1\amp k \\ 0\amp 1 \end{array} \right]\text{.}\))
