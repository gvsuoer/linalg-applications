Show that the set

\begin{equation*} \CB_n = \left\{\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}, \cos(x), \cos(2x), \ldots, \cos(nx), \sin(x), \sin(2x), \ldots, \sin(nx)\right\} \end{equation*}

is an orthonormal basis for \(W_n\text{.}\) Use the fact that the norm of a vector \(\vv\) in an inner product space with inner product \(\langle \ , \ \rangle\) is defined to be \(\sqrt{\langle \vv, \vv \rangle}\text{.}\) (This is where the factor of \(\frac{1}{\pi}\) will be helpful.)
