Now we need to recall how to find the best approximation to a vector by a vector in a subspace, and apply that idea to approximate an arbitrary function \(g\) with a trigonometric polynomial in \(W_n\text{.}\) Recall that the best approximation of a function \(g\) in \(C[-\pi,\pi]\) is the projection of \(g\) onto \(W_n\text{.}\) If we have an orthonormal basis \(\{h_0, h_1, h_2, \ldots, h_{2n}\}\) of \(W_n\text{,}\) then the projection of \(g\) onto \(W_n\) is

\begin{equation*} \proj_{W_n} g = \langle g,h_0 \rangle h_0 + \langle g,h_1 \rangle h_1 + \langle g,h_2 \rangle h_2 + \ldots + \langle g,h_{2n} \rangle h_{2n}\text{.} \end{equation*}
