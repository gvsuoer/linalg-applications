Let \(V\) be a vector space with basis \(\{\vv_1, \vv_2, \ldots, \vv_n\}\text{.}\) Define \(\langle \ , \ \rangle\) as follows:

\begin{equation*} \langle \vu, \vw \rangle = \sum_{i=1}^n u_iw_i \end{equation*}

if \(\vu = \sum_{i=1}^n u_i \vv_i\) and \(\vw = \sum_{i=1}^n w_i \vv_i\) in \(V\text{.}\) (Since the representation of a vector as a linear combination of basis elements is unique, this mapping is well-defined.) Show that \(\langle \ , \ \rangle\) is an inner product on \(V\) and conclude that any finite dimensional vector space can be made into an inner product space.
