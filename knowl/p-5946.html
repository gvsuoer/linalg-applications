TheoremÂ 34.3 can be extended to any linear combination of vectors by mathematical induction. That is, if \(T : V \to \R^n\) is the coordinate mapping defined by \(T(\vx) = [\vx]_{\CB}\) for some basis \(\CB\text{,}\) and if \(\vu_1\text{,}\) \(\vu_2\text{,}\) \(\ldots\text{,}\) \(\vu_m\) are vectors in an \(n\)-dimensional vector space \(V\) with basis \(\CB\text{,}\) and \(x_1\text{,}\) \(x_2\text{,}\) \(\ldots\text{,}\) \(x_m\) are scalars, then \(T(x_1\vu_1+x_2\vu_2+\cdots +x_m\vu_m) = x_1T(\vu_1) + x_2T(\vu_2) + \cdots + x_mT(\vu_m)\text{.}\) In other words,

\begin{equation*} [x_1\vu_1+x_2\vu_2+\cdots + x_m\vu_m]_{\CB} = x_1[\vu_1]_{\CB} + x_2[\vu_2]_{\CB} + \cdots + x_m[\vu_m]_{\CB}\text{.} \end{equation*}
