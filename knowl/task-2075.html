Task 35.9.d.

Now we demonstrate the uniqueness of this decomposition. Suppose \(\vx = \vw+\vw_1\) and \(\vx = \vu+\vu_1\text{,}\) where \(\vw\) and \(\vu\) are in \(W\) and \(\vw_1\) and \(\vu_1\) are in \(W^{\perp}\text{.}\) Show that \(\vw=\vu\) and \(\vw_1 = \vu_1\text{,}\) so that the representation of \(\vx\) as a sum of a vector in \(W\) and a vector in \(W^{\perp}\) is unique. (Hint: What is \(W \cap W^{\perp}\text{?}\))
